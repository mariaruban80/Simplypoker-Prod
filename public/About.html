What is Simply Poker?

Planning Poker is a consensus-based estimation technique used by agile teams to estimate the complexity of user stories or tasks. It brings together multiple perspectives from your team members, creating more accurate estimates while fostering engaging discussions about requirements.

Our digital Simply Poker app brings this collaborative experience online, allowing distributed teams to estimate together in real-time—whether you're in the office or working remotely from anywhere in the world.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Planning Poker and why should we use it?

Planning Poker is a consensus-based estimation technique used by agile teams. Team members make estimates individually, reducing influence bias. It's effective because it:

  • Brings multiple perspectives into the estimation process
  • Reduces anchoring bias by revealing all votes simultaneously
  • Encourages discussion when estimates differ
  • Creates team ownership of the estimates
  • Makes the estimation process more engaging and interactive
How many people can join a Planning Poker session?

The Simply Poker app supports teams of all sizes. We've optimized for 3-12 participants, which is typical for most agile teams, but larger groups are supported as well. The interface automatically adapts to accommodate the number of participants in your session.

Is my data stored on your servers?

Session data is temporarily stored only for the duration of the active session. We don't permanently store your stories or votes. For added privacy, you can export your results at the end of the session if you need to keep a record.

What's the difference between the various voting scales?

Different teams prefer different scales based on their workflow:

  • Fibonacci (0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21...): Reflects the uncertainty in larger estimates with bigger gaps between higher numbers.
  • Short Fibonacci (0, ½, 1, 2, 3): Better for teams working with smaller, more granular stories.
  • T-Shirt Sizes (XXS to XXL): Intuitive for teams that prefer non-numeric, relative sizing.
  • T-Shirt & Numbers: Combines the intuitiveness of t-shirt sizes with numeric values for easier tracking.
How can I import my backlog from Jira or other tools?

Export your stories from Jira or other tools as a CSV file, then use the "Upload Ticket" feature to import them. The CSV format should have one story per line, with details separated by commas. After your planning session, you can export the estimates back to update your project management tool.

What happens if someone gets disconnected during a session?

The app automatically attempts to reconnect users who lose their connection. When they rejoin, they'll see the current state of the session and their previous votes will be preserved, ensuring a smooth experience even with occasional connectivity issues.

Can I customize the estimation scales?

Yes, in addition to the pre-defined scales (Fibonacci, Short Fibonacci, T-shirt sizes, and T-shirt sizes with numbers), you can also select the Custom option to define your own estimation values that best fit your team's needs.
